# Standard Playbook (Sample)

This file is the source of truth for the playbook RAG. Each `## heading`
becomes a separately-retrievable section. Edit positions to match your
organization's standards and re-run "(Re)build playbook index" in the UI.

## Limitation of Liability

**Approved position:** Aggregate liability cap of fees paid in the 12 months
preceding the claim. Mutual cap. Standard exclusions (gross negligence,
willful misconduct, IP indemnity, breach of confidentiality, breach of data
protection obligations) are carved out. No consequential, indirect, special,
or punitive damages on either side, but lost profits flowing from a direct
breach are recoverable.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Uncapped liability for the customer beyond the carve-outs.
- One-sided cap that protects only the vendor.
- Cap below 6 months of fees.

**Talking points:** A 12-month fees cap is market for SaaS and services; an
uncapped indemnity for IP infringement is acceptable; uncapped general
liability is not.

## Indemnification

**Approved position:** Vendor indemnifies customer against third-party IP
infringement claims arising from use of the service as authorized. Customer
indemnifies vendor against misuse of the service or breach of its
obligations. Indemnification procedure: prompt written notice, sole control
of defense by indemnifying party, customer cooperation, no settlement that
admits liability without consent.

**Walkaway terms:**
- One-way indemnity benefiting vendor only.
- Indemnity that excludes IP claims.
- No defense control or notice requirements.

## Confidentiality

**Approved position:** Mutual confidentiality. Five-year survival from
disclosure for ordinary confidential information, perpetual for trade
secrets. Standard exclusions (publicly available, independently developed,
rightfully obtained from a third party, required by law with notice).
Reasonable care standard, no less than the receiving party uses for its
own confidential information.

**Walkaway terms:**
- One-way confidentiality.
- Survival shorter than three years.
- No carve-out for legally required disclosure.

## Termination

**Approved position:** Either party may terminate for material breach
uncured after 30 days' written notice. Termination for convenience permitted
by either party with 90 days' notice; pro-rata refund of prepaid fees on
termination for vendor breach or convenience by vendor; no refund on
customer termination for convenience. Termination for insolvency permitted.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Termination for breach with cure period under 14 days.
- No customer-side termination for convenience.
- Forfeiture of prepaid fees on any termination.

## Governing Law

**Approved position:** Governing law of the customer's state (or, for
non-US customers, English law). Exclusive jurisdiction in courts of the
customer's principal place of business. No mandatory arbitration.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Governing law of a jurisdiction where neither party operates.
- Mandatory arbitration in a remote venue.

## Intellectual Property

**Approved position:** Vendor retains all IP in the service and any
improvements. Customer retains all IP in customer data and any customer-
provided materials. Customer grants vendor a limited license to use customer
data only as necessary to provide the service. Feedback is licensed to
vendor on a non-exclusive, royalty-free basis but does not transfer
ownership of customer trade secrets.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Vendor claims ownership of customer data or derivatives.
- Vendor claims ownership of customer feedback as outright assignment.

## Data Protection

**Approved position:** Vendor acts as processor of customer personal data.
DPA incorporated by reference. Standard contractual clauses for cross-border
transfers. Vendor implements industry-standard security measures (encryption
in transit and at rest, access controls, regular penetration testing).
Notification of personal data breach within 72 hours of discovery.

**Walkaway terms:**
- No DPA.
- Breach notification longer than 72 hours.
- No contractual security commitments.

## Warranty

**Approved position:** Vendor warrants the service will perform materially
in accordance with documentation and will be provided in a professional and
workmanlike manner. Sole remedy for breach of the performance warranty:
re-performance or, if re-performance is not feasible, termination and
pro-rata refund. All other warranties disclaimed to the extent permitted
by law.

**Walkaway terms:**
- All warranties disclaimed including performance warranty.
- Sole remedy capped below the disputed fees.

## Payment Terms

**Approved position:** Net 30 from invoice date. Late charges at 1.5% per
month or the maximum permitted by law, whichever is lower. Disputed
amounts must be raised in writing within 30 days of invoice; undisputed
portions remain payable on time.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Net 7 or less.
- Late charges above 2% per month.
- No dispute mechanism.

## Non-Compete

**Approved position:** No non-compete in commercial supply or services
contracts unless narrowly tailored to a specific project or customer
relationship and limited to 12 months. Employee non-competes follow
applicable jurisdiction rules; not enforceable in California, etc.

**Walkaway terms:**
- Non-compete that prevents customer from working with any vendor competitor.
- Duration over 24 months.
